## Executive Summary

The specification and plan are sound, well-bounded, and fully aligned with the project
constitution — this slice is the textbook embodiment of Principle XVII (All-Python
Toolchain Boundary) and correctly confines all change to the web shell (Principle XVIII).
There are no must-address findings and no constitutional conflicts. The strongest part is
scope discipline: the throwaway demo, the explicit hand-offs to issues #171/#172/#166, and
the founder-approved production-serving decision are all crisp. The most valuable
improvements are defensive hardening rather than corrections: (1) the missing-`collectstatic`
failure mode has a wider blast radius than the plan implies (it breaks *every* static-using
page in production, not just the demo), so the hand-off to the deploy task (#177) deserves to
be a verification gate, not just a note; (2) Principle II (Hook-First Enforcement) argues the
no-build-step invariant should eventually be a deterministic pre-commit hook rather than the
spec's probabilistic check; and (3) because this slice sets the htmx pattern every later page
will copy, it should explicitly establish the XSS-safe fragment convention now. All are
recommendations; none blocks proceeding.
