# Trade-off Analysis

## What it is

Trade-off analysis is the systematic process of evaluating the competing costs, benefits, and opportunity costs of options to determine the best allocation of limited resources. It is not a single model but an umbrella concept executed through tools such as cost-benefit analysis and the Project Management Triangle.

## Core principle

You cannot have it all. Every choice carries a cost -- the value of the best alternative you did not choose. Optimising for every dimension simultaneously is impossible, so you must deliberately decide what to sacrifice.

## When to invoke

- Navigating complex decisions where multiple goals compete (speed vs quality vs cost)
- Allocating scarce resources: time, budget, attention
- Managing project constraints where stakeholder expectations clash with reality
- Evaluating a design option that improves one attribute at the expense of another

## How to apply

1. List all options and identify their explicit and implicit costs, including intangibles
2. Assign relative or dollar values to each cost and benefit -- do not skip intangibles
3. Arrange costs and benefits on a timeline to visualise near-term vs long-term impact
4. Run a sensitivity analysis: vary key assumptions and observe how much they shift the outcome
5. Apply the Project Management Triangle: accept that you can optimise at most two of Good, Fast, Cheap -- make a deliberate, named sacrifice on the third

## Anti-patterns

- **Garbage in, garbage out** -- feeding the analysis with vague estimates guarantees a flawed conclusion; imprecise estimates are still better than none
- **Ignoring intangibles** -- omitting hard-to-price factors (reputation, stress, team morale) skews the comparison and conceals real costs
- **The perfect trap** -- refusing to make deliberate trade-offs and expecting all three constraints to be satisfied simultaneously
