# OODA Loop

## What it is

The OODA Loop (Observe, Orient, Decide, Act) is a rapid, continuous four-step decision-making cycle originally developed for military fighter pilots. It functions as a feedback loop where you constantly take in information, reassess the situation, choose a course of action, and execute.

## Core principle

The individual or organisation that moves through the loop fastest will learn faster, make better decisions, and ultimately win. Speed of adaptation is the competitive advantage.

## When to invoke

- Highly competitive, uncertain, or rapidly changing situations
- Racing competitors for product/market fit
- Navigating a dynamic, unfolding landscape (technology, markets, operations)
- Need to outmanoeuvre an adversary or respond to their moves in real time

## How to apply

1. **Observe** -- take a quick glance at the changing environment and incorporate new external information
2. **Orient** -- immediately update your assessment of the situation based on what you observed
3. **Decide** -- determine the next best course of action
4. **Act** -- execute the decision without hesitation

After acting, repeat the loop immediately to observe results and react to counter-moves.

## Anti-patterns

- **Hesitation and over-analysis** -- stopping to deliberate at length destroys momentum and lets the competition outmanoeuvre you; the loop was designed for environments where there is no time for analysis between actions
- **Using it in stable environments** -- applying a rapid action-biased loop to situations that demand slow, methodical, irreversible decision-making is a mismatch
