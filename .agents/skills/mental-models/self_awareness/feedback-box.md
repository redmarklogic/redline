# The Feedback Box

## What it is

The Feedback Box is a two-axis matrix that categorises compliments and criticisms you receive so you can establish a clear plan of action. Instead of viewing feedback as simply "good" or "bad", it maps each piece against what you thought and what needs to change.

## Core principle

The question is not "was this good or bad?" but "what can I do with this feedback?". Categorising feedback by actionability prevents emotional reactions from driving your response.

## When to invoke

- Receiving compliments or criticisms and need to decide rationally how to react
- Post-review or performance feedback session
- Evaluating peer or stakeholder input on a deliverable
- Need to separate luck from genuine skill in your outcomes

## How to apply

1. Receive feedback and plot it into one of four quadrants:
   - **Advice** -- you thought it was good, but it needs to change
   - **Compliment** -- you thought it was good, and it can stay as is
   - **Criticism** -- you thought it was bad, and it has to change
   - **Suggestion** -- you thought it was bad, but you can live with it
2. Honestly ask: "which success or failure was actually due to luck?" to filter out chance outcomes
3. Use the categorisation to decide which advice to follow, which criticisms demand action, and which suggestions to set aside

## Anti-patterns

- **Complacency from false praise** -- letting unearned compliments make you comfortable instead of questioning whether the outcome was skill or luck
- **Taking criticism personally** -- letting negative feedback damage self-esteem and trigger emotional rather than rational responses
- **Confusing luck with skill** -- accepting praise for a success that was actually a lucky break without evaluating the feedback objectively
