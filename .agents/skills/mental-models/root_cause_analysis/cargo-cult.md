# Cargo Cult

## What it is

A Cargo Cult is the practice of faithfully reproducing the visible forms of a successful process -- the rituals, structures, and appearances -- without understanding the underlying mechanics that actually make it work.

## Core principle

Mimicking the surface of a practice without grasping the causal mechanism produces no real results. Understanding *why* something works is a prerequisite for successfully replicating it.

## When to invoke

- Copying code patterns, templates, or "best practices" from examples without verifying they fit the current context
- A borrowed approach fails to produce the expected results despite following it exactly
- Reviewing a process that was adopted because "that is how the other team does it"
- Evaluating whether a convention is justified by first principles or merely inherited

## How to apply

1. When adopting a pattern, template, or practice, ask "why does this work in the original context?"
2. Identify the causal mechanism -- the specific conditions that make the pattern effective
3. Check whether those conditions hold in your context
4. If they do not, adapt the pattern to fit your actual situation rather than copying it verbatim
5. If you cannot identify the causal mechanism, treat the adoption as an experiment and validate outcomes

## Anti-patterns

- **Blind adoption** -- implementing a pattern because it is popular or because a respected source recommended it, without understanding the preconditions
- **Ritual over substance** -- following all the ceremony of a process (standups, retros, sprint planning) while ignoring the principles that make it effective
- **Dismissing all patterns** -- the opposite extreme; proven patterns are valuable when applied with understanding
