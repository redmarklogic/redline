# Inversion

## What it is

Inversion is the practice of approaching a problem from the opposite end of its natural starting point. Instead of asking "how do I succeed?", you flip the question and ask "how would I guarantee failure?" then work to eliminate those causes.

## Core principle

Avoiding stupidity is easier than seeking brilliance. You can more reliably achieve your goal by identifying and removing guaranteed-failure behaviours than by trying to find the one perfect strategy.

## When to invoke

- Stuck on a difficult problem where forward-thinking approaches are fruitless
- Trying to implement change but facing hidden resistance
- Operating in competitive domains where minimising losses matters more than maximising gains
- Planning a project and want to stress-test assumptions

## How to apply

1. Define your objective clearly
2. Identify the supporting forces -- actions needed to achieve the goal
3. Invert the goal -- ask "how could I guarantee a terrible outcome?"
4. List the impeding forces -- behaviours, obstacles, or decisions that would guarantee failure
5. Act to systematically avoid the negatives and remove the obstacles you identified

Alternatively: assume the desired endpoint is already true, then work backward to deduce what else must be true to make it a reality.

## Anti-patterns

- **Stopping at the forward-thinking stage** -- identifying what you need to succeed but never considering what could make the situation worse
- **Chasing magical solutions** -- wasting energy on "brilliant" strategies instead of avoiding obvious negative behaviours (e.g. trying to beat the market rather than minimising fees)
