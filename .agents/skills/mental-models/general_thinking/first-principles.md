# First Principles Thinking

## What it is

First Principles Thinking is a method of reverse-engineering complex situations by separating underlying facts from assumptions, then rebuilding solutions from irreducible, foundational truths rather than reasoning by analogy or pattern-matching.

## Core principle

Challenge inherited patterns and conventional wisdom. Decompose a problem down to its most basic, provably true elements and construct your solution upward from those elements alone.

## When to invoke

- Pattern-matching fails or produces nonsensical results
- Entering an unfamiliar domain where existing templates may not apply
- Existing solutions feel cargo-culted or inherited without justification
- Seeking a fundamentally different approach rather than an incremental tweak

## How to apply

1. Identify the problem or assumption you want to challenge
2. Break it down -- ask "what do I know to be true?" and discard everything that is merely assumed or inherited
3. Use Socratic questioning: "why do we do it this way?", "what evidence supports this?", "what if the opposite were true?"
4. Rebuild a solution from only the verified foundational truths
5. Validate the new solution against reality before committing

## Anti-patterns

- **Reasoning by analogy** -- copying what worked elsewhere without verifying the underlying conditions match
- **Stopping too early** -- accepting "because that is how it is done" as a foundational truth when it is actually an assumption
- **Over-applying** -- using first principles on trivial decisions where pattern-matching is efficient and sufficient
