# Marketing — LinkedIn Social Selling

Source: `Digital Marketing & Social Selling` notebook.

## Boundary Contract

## Core Principle

B2B buyers buy from people they trust before they buy from companies. LinkedIn
social selling is the discipline of building familiarity with prospects over weeks
and months **without aggressive pitching**, so that when they do have a need, your
team is the first they think of.

**Slow prospecting beats cold outreach.** Comments build relationships; cold DMs
burn them.

## When to Use

- Onboarding a founder or sales rep onto LinkedIn
- Auditing an existing LinkedIn profile that isn't generating inbound
- Designing a prospecting campaign for a defined ICP segment
- Comment volume on Redline posts is flat and prospects aren't engaging back

## The Three Pillars

## Daily Ritual: Social Selling Time-Block

30-60 minutes per day, same time each day. Order:

1. Check notifications, respond to comments and DMs on Redline content.
2. Open the active Sales Navigator saved list.
3. Leave 10 thoughtful comments on prospects' recent posts (LCS rule).
4. **Optional:** publish 1 original post (if it's a publish day per the editorial
   calendar).
5. Log new connections and noteworthy conversations in the CRM.

## Procedure for a New Team-Member Onboarding

1. **Profile audit.** Score current profile against PIPA. Identify gaps.
2. **Rewrite About section** using PIPA. Match Redline brand voice.
3. **Update headline + banner** to reinforce positioning.
4. **Build the Sales Navigator list** for their ICP segment (with the Strategy Advisor's input on ICP).
5. **Run a 2-week shadow.** New team-member shadows the founder's daily ritual.
6. **Cut them loose** after 2 weeks with a weekly review for the first month.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Sending a connection request with a pitch in the same message | Connect first with a personalised note; send the value offer only after the connection is accepted |
| Posting company content without a personal founder angle | LinkedIn reach is personal; reframe company news from the founder's perspective and experience |
| Treating every comment as a direct sales opportunity | Comment to add insight, not to promote — the goal is visibility and trust, not immediate conversion |
