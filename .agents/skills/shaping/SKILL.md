# Shaping

## Purpose

Shape Up shaping process adapted for Redline's pipeline: Discovery to Shaping to Execution to Evaluation. Translates product intent into buildable scope before work enters SpecKit.

## What This Skill Covers

- Shape Up shaping process adapted for Redline's pipeline: Discovery to Shaping to Execution to Evaluation
- Pitch format: what a shaped brief contains (problem statement, appetite, solution sketch at breadboard level, rabbit holes identified, no-gos)
- Breadboarding technique: components and connections only, no visual design, no wireframes, "fat marker sketches"
- Rabbit hole identification: deeply complex technical unknowns or misunderstood interdependencies; resolved before work reaches SpecKit
- Appetite setting: Mark sets business appetite (how much time the business is willing to invest), Peter sets technical appetite (what's technically feasible within that time)
- Scope hammering: what to cut when the shaped work doesn't fit the appetite
- Two-Touch model: Touch 1 (constraints memo / Pitch, pre-design) to Matt designs freely to SpecKit specifies to Touch 2 (Peter reviews SpecKit output for architectural compliance, not design specs)
- What shaping is NOT: shaping is not wireframing, not detailed specification, not UX design, not prescribing solutions
- Output: shaped Pitch written to `specs/shaped/`

## Key Boundary

The Pitch communicates constraints and scope. Solutions live within the team's creative freedom. If Peter's constraint can only be satisfied by one specific visual design, Peter has over-constrained.
