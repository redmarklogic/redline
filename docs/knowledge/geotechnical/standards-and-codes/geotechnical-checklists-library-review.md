## Key Facts

### The Core Domain Distinction

1. **Authoring guidance** tells a practitioner how to structure, draft, and present a geotechnical
   report — section content, factual vs. interpretive delineation, communication standards. A
   practitioner reaches for this at the start of a report.

2. **Review and QC checklists** tell a checker, regulator, or client what to look for when
   assessing a report that already exists. They are diagnostic tools applied after the fact.

3. These two purposes are functionally distinct and serve different moments in the geotechnical
   work lifecycle. Conflating them in a single notebook degrades retrieval precision.
