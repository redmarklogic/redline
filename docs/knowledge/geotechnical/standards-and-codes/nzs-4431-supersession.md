# NZS 4431 — Supersession History and Rule Engine Nomination

**Sub-domain**: standards-and-codes
**Last verified**: 2026-05-14
**Confidence**: cross-referenced (supersession confirmed by notebook; technical delta is practice-grounded)
**Sources**: Engineering Standards notebook (CCC Construction Standard Specification 2025 Amendments Summary)

## Summary

NZS 4431:1989 (*Code of Practice for Earth Fill for Residential Purposes*) was the sole NZ
standard for residential earthworks compaction for 33 years. It was superseded by NZS 4431:2022,
which introduced liquefaction susceptibility criteria for fill materials following the
Canterbury earthquake sequence. The 1989 edition is one of the highest-frequency citation
errors in NZ residential geotechnical reports because engineers' document templates have
contained it since 1989.

## Key Facts

1. **NZS 4431:1989** (*Code of Practice for Earth Fill for Residential Purposes*) was the
   active edition from 1989 to 2022 — 33 uninterrupted years [Engineering Standards, sources 1 & 2].

2. **NZS 4431:2022** is the current edition. The Christchurch City Council Construction
   Standard Specification (2025 Amendments Summary) formally updated their mandatory
   reference from the 1989 to the 2022 edition in October 2025 [Engineering Standards, source 3].

3. The exact formal SNZ withdrawal date for the 1989 edition is **not confirmed in the
   notebooks**. It should be verified against the SNZ catalogue before use in production
   rule metadata.

4. The exact formal title of the 2022 edition varies slightly across sources. The CCC uses
   "CoP for earth fill for residential development". The SNZ catalogue title should be
   confirmed before production.

5. NZS 4431 appears as a mandatory referenced document in CCC infrastructure design
   standards and subdivision consent documentation — it is one of the most frequently
   cited NZ-specific standards in residential geotechnical reports [Engineering Standards,
   sources 1 & 2].

## Technical Delta Between Editions (Practice-Grounded — Not Notebook-Sourced)

The following is based on 25 years of NZ geotechnical practice experience, not notebook
sources. It should be corroborated against the 2022 standard text before use in marketing
or product copy.

The substantive change in the 2022 edition is the introduction of **fill liquefaction
susceptibility criteria**. The Canterbury earthquake sequence (2010–2011) demonstrated
that fill compacted to the 1989 specification could liquefy under design earthquake loading,
causing widespread residential building damage. The 2022 edition requires engineers to
assess whether fill material is susceptible to liquefaction and to select or treat fill
accordingly.

The 1989 edition specifies compaction to a percentage of maximum dry density (MDD) but
contains no requirement to assess seismic liquefaction behaviour of the fill material
itself. An engineer following the 1989 edition perfectly would not perform a liquefaction
susceptibility assessment — the requirement simply does not exist in that edition.

### Severity Rationale

MEDIUM was proposed by Mark. Graeme recommends HIGH on the following grounds:

1. The omission is substantive, not administrative. The 1989 edition lacks fill
   liquefaction requirements that are fundamental to post-Canterbury NZ practice.
2. NZ is seismically active. The general NZ context does not support treating the
   two editions as practically equivalent.
3. Citing a superseded standard in a report supporting a Building Act Producer Statement
   creates professional liability exposure that a competent peer reviewer will flag.
4. The remediation effort is minimal (single citation update), so HIGH severity is not
   disproportionate.
