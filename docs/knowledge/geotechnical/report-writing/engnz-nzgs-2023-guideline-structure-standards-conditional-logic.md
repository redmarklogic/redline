## Summary

The ENZ/NZGS (Engineering New Zealand / New Zealand Geotechnical Society) Geotechnical Reports
Guideline and Template (August 2023) covers one report type only: the **Geotechnical Assessment
Report**, scoped to simple, low-risk, and typical residential development projects.
Geotechnical Basis of Design Reports (GBR) — whether read as "Basis of Design" or as
"Baseline Report" — are explicitly out of scope. The guideline specifies a 12-section template
structure and contains seven identifiable conditional rules that govern which sections or content
blocks are included, excluded, or modified depending on project-specific factors.

## Key Facts

### GBR-STRUCT-01 — GBR Coverage

1. The guideline does **not** cover Geotechnical Basis of Design Reports. The term does not appear
   in the document. [Source: 4cc36535-1458-47d8-a7bb-413ca45995f8, citation 1–23]

2. Geotechnical Baseline Reports (GBR — the contractually binding subsurface risk document used in
   construction contracts) are also not covered. The guideline's single in-scope report type is the
   **Geotechnical Assessment Report**. [Source: 4cc36535-1458-47d8-a7bb-413ca45995f8, citation 11]

3. Scope statement (verbatim): "Engineering New Zealand…has produced this guideline and report
   template to be used by engineers to communicate geotechnical findings to other disciplines on
   **simple, low-risk projects**." [Source: 4cc36535-1458-47d8-a7bb-413ca45995f8, citation 1]

4. The scope is further refined to "**typical residential development projects**."
   [Source: 4cc36535-1458-47d8-a7bb-413ca45995f8, citation 2]

5. The template is "a communication tool and not a substitute for professional engineering advice or
   judgement." [Source: 4cc36535-1458-47d8-a7bb-413ca45995f8, citation 2]

6. The 12 primary headings specified for the Geotechnical Assessment Report are:
   1. Introduction (scope, parties involved, purpose, construction monitoring engagement)
   2. Site description (slope, elevation, services, neighbouring sites, proposed development)
   3. Area geology (3.1 publicly available geological mapping; 3.2 regional geology and seismicity)
   4. Review of available information (4.1 past earthquakes and precedent performance; 4.2 existing
      geotechnical records incl. NZGD; 4.3 flooding; 4.4 summary of previous geotechnical reports)
   5. Geotechnical investigations (5.1 previous investigations; 5.2 current investigations)
   6. Subsurface conditions (6.1 ground model and soil profile; 6.2 groundwater; 6.3 rationale for
      parameters; 6.4 uncertainties and assumptions; 6.5 site subsoil class per NZS 1170.5)
   7. Geotechnical hazards (7.1–7.15 — all conditional; see CL-2023-01 and Section 7 Sub-section Titles below)
   8. Geotechnical recommendations (8.1 Safety in Design; 8.2 Foundations; 8.3 Filling and
      earthworks; 8.4 Retaining walls; 8.5 Slope design; 8.6 Other engineering considerations)
   9. Conclusions
   10. Limitations
   11. References
   12. Appendices (drawings, investigation logs, analysis outputs, cross sections, Safety in Design
       risk register, statement of professional opinion)
   [Source: 4cc36535-1458-47d8-a7bb-413ca45995f8, citations 14–23]

### CL-2023-01 — Conditional and Branching Logic

The guideline states explicitly: "It's unlikely that every part of the template below will be
exactly applicable to each project." [Source: 4cc36535-1458-47d8-a7bb-413ca45995f8, citation 1]

Seven conditional rules are identifiable from the document:

**Rule 1 — Section 7 (Geotechnical Hazards): overall section applicability**
- Condition: depends on the nature of the site and the proposed development
- Trigger: INCLUDE sub-sections 7.1–7.15 only "as appropriate to the site and development"
- Stated: EXPLICIT — verbatim tag on Section 7 heading [citation 2]

**Rule 2 — Section 7 (specific hazard analysis): detailed analysis present**
- Condition: if analysis or detailed assessment is undertaken (e.g. liquefaction analysis)
- Trigger: INCLUDE the full details of that analysis within Section 7; this is mandatory, not
  optional — "the details must be included"
- Stated: EXPLICIT [citation 2]

**Rule 3 — Section 8.2 (Foundations): site and development applicability**
- Condition: depends on the nature of the site and proposed development
- Trigger: INCLUDE Section 8.2 and its sub-sections (8.2.1–8.2.9) only "as appropriate to the
  site and development"; detailed foundation design is flagged as likely outside scope and to
  be provided in a separate report
- Stated: EXPLICIT — tag "as appropriate to the site and development" on 8.2 heading [citation 3–4]

**Rule 4 — Section 8.3 (Filling and Earthworks): site and development applicability**
- Condition: depends on the nature of the site and proposed development
- Trigger: INCLUDE Section 8.3 only "as appropriate to the site and development"
- Stated: EXPLICIT — verbatim tag on 8.3 heading [citation 4]

**Rule 5 — Section 8.4 (Retaining Walls): existing or proposed walls present**
- Condition: if site has existing retaining walls, or if new walls are proposed as an option
- Trigger: INCLUDE assessment of existing walls and parameters for new walls "as options
  discussion if appropriate"
- Stated: EXPLICIT — "as appropriate to the site and development" tag on 8.4 heading; sub-text
  "Include an assessment of existing walls and parameters for new walls as options discussion if
  appropriate" [citation 5]

**Rule 6 — Section 4.3 (Flooding): uncertainty in available flooding information**
- Condition: if flooding information/assessment is uncertain
- Trigger: MODIFY Section 4.3 content to include "recommendations for specialist assessment if
  considered necessary"
- Stated: EXPLICIT — "If uncertain, this should include recommendations for specialist
  assessment if considered necessary" [citation 6]

**Rule 7 — Section 12 Appendices (site plan footprint and statement of professional opinion)**
- Condition (a): if site plan footprint of proposed works is available at the time of reporting
- Trigger: INCLUDE the footprint on the site plan drawing in appendices; if not yet available,
  exclude
- Stated: EXPLICIT — "site plan showing footprint of proposed works (if available at the time of
  reporting)" [citation 7]

- Condition (b): if a statement of professional opinion is required by client or authority
- Trigger: INCLUDE a statement of professional opinion in appendices
- Stated: EXPLICIT — "Statement of professional opinion: as required" [citation 8]

**Rule 8 — Local regulatory or region-specific variables (author-added sections)**
- Condition: if project is subject to local regulatory requirements or region-specific variables
  not covered by the baseline template
- Trigger: INCLUDE (add) additional sections authored by the report writer; the generator must
  not prevent this
- Stated: EXPLICIT — "the report's author is responsible for adding sections as appropriate so
  that these aspects are appropriately addressed" [citation 1]

## Open Questions

1. The guideline's "as appropriate to the site and development" qualifier appears on Sections 7,
   8.2, 8.3, and 8.4. The document does not define which project inputs should trigger inclusion
   of each section — that judgment is left entirely to the practitioner. The skeleton generator
   will need to define its own decision logic (e.g. dropdown inputs) to operationalise these
   triggers. This is a product design decision, not one answerable from the document.

2. The full sub-section list for Sections 8.5 (Slope design) and 8.6 (Other engineering
   considerations) does not carry the "as appropriate" tag. It is not clear from the document
   whether these sub-sections are always mandatory or also conditional. Inferred as conditional
   given the overall framing — but not stated explicitly.

3. The guideline targets simple, low-risk, residential projects. The skeleton generator's PRD
   needs to confirm whether the tool is scoped to this population only, or whether it must
   handle more complex project types for which this guideline is insufficient.
