# Audit Trail Sign-Off Workflows in Geotechnical Consultancies

**Sub-domain**: report-writing
**Last verified**: 2026-05-05
**Confidence**: cross-referenced
**Sources**: Geotechnical Report Workflows, Risk Assessment in Engineering

## Summary

Report sign-off in geotechnical consultancies follows a multi-tier chain (Author, Technical
Reviewer, Project Director) in large firms, but compresses to effectively two-tier or
single-signer in smaller firms. Issuing a report is a discrete, formally governed event with
specific gatekeeping artefacts (review checklists, signature blocks, document registers).
Sign-off is at the document level in standard single-discipline reports; section-level
review occurs only on large multi-disciplinary projects.

## Key Facts

1. Large consultancies mandate a three-tier sign-off chain: Author (Prepared by), Technical
   Reviewer (Reviewed by), and Project Director (Authorised by). The firm's Six Key Business
   Rules require that "the project team structure has independence between output creation
   and oversight of outcomes" and "the PM and PD must be different people"
   [Geotechnical Report Workflows].

2. The TR verifies "the right answer has been given to the right question." The PD provides
   commercial and risk oversight, confirms client needs met, mandatory caveats included, and
   all prior reviews completed [Geotechnical Report Workflows].

3. For high-risk or high-value projects (>$10M), sign-off escalates to Discipline Director,
   Sector Director, Managing Director, and Board of Directors
   [Geotechnical Report Workflows].

4. A Professional Engineer's Seal must be "affixed, signed, and dated on every final report"
   and an engineer must not sign reports "unless actually prepared by him or prepared under
   his supervision" [Risk Assessment in Engineering].

5. Issuing a report is a discrete event requiring: signature block signed by Author/TR/PD,
   PD sign-off on Section B Review Checklist ("Approved for issue as Final"), PDF publication,
   and filing in "Issued Documents" folder [Geotechnical Report Workflows].

6. An Information Register records all outgoing deliverables: version, author, date sent,
   and recipients [Geotechnical Report Workflows].

7. In standard single-discipline reports (GIR/GIntR), the TR and PD review the "complete
   deliverable" -- sign-off is document-level [Geotechnical Report Workflows].

8. Section-level technical review occurs on multi-disciplinary projects where different
   Technical Directors review their respective discipline sections, but a single PD authorises
   the overall package [Geotechnical Report Workflows].

9. Medium firms (20-50 staff) typically operate two-tier in practice: the PD often doubles
   as the TR. Small firms (5-15 staff) are effectively single-signer, with the principal
   reviewing and signing (practitioner experience).

10. "Establishing a procedure for review of draft and final reports is an important defence
    against professional liability claims" [Risk Assessment in Engineering].

11. "Cryptic undated notes on scraps of paper... can be used by an opposing lawyer to create
    an air of uncertainty, if not incompetence" [Risk Assessment in Engineering].
