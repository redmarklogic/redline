## Key Facts

### (a) NZGS Earthquake Geotechnical Engineering Practice — Module 1

1. The NZGS Earthquake Geotechnical Engineering Practice series has six modules. Module 1 is
   titled "Overview of the Guidelines." It introduces the module series, provides context within
   NZ's building regulatory framework, and covers ground motion parameters for geotechnical
   design. It does NOT contain guidance on geotechnical report structuring, factual/interpretive
   delineation, or section-by-section authoring. Its purpose is regulatory orientation and
   seismic hazard overview — not report writing.

2. The original acquisition target note in the register listed "NZGS Module 1 (Site
   Characterisation)." No NZGS earthquake module is titled "Site Characterisation." Module 2
   is the nearest match: "Geotechnical Investigations for Earthquake Engineering." Neither
   Module 1 nor Module 2 is the right source for report structuring and authoring guidance.

3. The correct NZGS-adjacent publication for NZ geotechnical report structure is the
   Engineering New Zealand / NZGS joint document: "Geotechnical Reports Guideline and
   Template," August 2023. This is the authoritative NZ practitioner guide for report
   structuring, section-by-section content, and communication standards for geotechnical
   reports, aimed at residential and low-risk projects. It is freely available online.

4. The 2016 Module 1 (Overview) is still current in its 2016 version at NZGS.org and
   building.govt.nz. Revised modules were released November 2021, but the revisions applied
   to substantive technical modules (liquefaction, foundations, etc.) — the overview module
   retains its 2016 date.

### (b) CEDD Hong Kong GeoGuides — Report Structuring and Authoring Relevance

5. There are seven GeoGuides in total (GeoGuide 1–7). For the report-workflows notebook
   purpose, the relevant ones are:

   - **GeoGuide 2**: "Guide to Site Investigation" (2017 edition, freely downloadable, 16.4 MB).
     This is the primary acquisition target. It contains Part VI titled "Reports and
     Interpretation," which directly addresses site investigation report structure. It aligns
     with BS 5930 (the UK standard for site investigation). It is a strong match for
     factual/interpretive delineation and report authoring guidance.

   - **GeoGuide 3**: "Guide to Rock and Soil Descriptions" (2017 edition). This is the companion
     document to GeoGuide 2 — it covers Section 8 of BS 5930 (description standards). It is
     relevant to factual content quality in reports (how to describe soils and rock correctly)
     but is not a report-structuring guide per se. Useful secondary source.

6. GeoGuides 1, 4, 5, 6, and 7 are topic-specific design guides (retaining walls, caverns,
   slope maintenance, reinforced fill, soil nails). They do not address report writing,
   structuring, or factual/interpretive delineation. They should NOT be ingested into the
   report-workflows notebook.

7. GEO Reports (the numbered research report series, distinct from GeoGuides) are individual
   investigation or research reports — they are source material for technical topics, not
   authoring guidance. None are relevant to this notebook's purpose.
