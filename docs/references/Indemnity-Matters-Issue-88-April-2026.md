## 1. Professionals and AI (page 4)

The newsletter introduced this section as follows:

> "The following articles, whilst a few months old, reflect how some of our fellow
> professionals, in this case accountants and lawyers, have been found wanting in their
> use of artificial intelligence (AI). They provide cautionary tales for those using AI
> in their business: **how open are you with your clientele about your use of AI? And
> are your reports factually correct?**"

**Why it matters for Redline**: This question was posed by the engineers' own PI
insurance provider — not a tech blog. It is a direct signal that the insurance industry
is beginning to scrutinise AI usage in professional reports.

### Deloitte (accountants)

Deloitte was required to partially refund a A$440,000 government report after admitting
generative AI was used to produce it and errors were found. A Labor senator described it
as "a human intelligence problem."

Source article: https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/2025/oct/06/deloitte-to-pay-money-back-to-albanese-government-after-using-ai-in-440000-report

### WA Lawyer

A Western Australian lawyer was referred to the legal regulator after submitting
AI-generated court documents that cited cases that do not exist.

> "It is one of more than 20 cases so far in Australia in which AI use has resulted in
> fake citations or other errors in court submissions."

The judge noted the incident "demonstrates the inherent dangers associated with
practitioners solely relying on the use of artificial intelligence."

Source article: https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/2025/aug/20/wa-lawyer-referred-to-regulator-after-preparing-documents-with-ai-generated-case-citations-that-did-not-exist-ntwnfb

## 2. PS4 Practice Advisory (page 3)

CEAS (with Engineering NZ and ACE NZ) issued a practice advisory titled *"Defining scope
of construction monitoring"* in October 2025, following concerns from Building Consent
Authorities about altered PS4 (Producer Statement — Construction Review) documents
submitted for Code Compliance Certificate applications. This followed an August 2025
practice alert and a December 2024 advisory on the Bella Vista Court of Appeal judgment.

The advisory is available in the Members' area of the CEAS website under
Guides and Manuals.

**Why it matters for Redline**: Three PS4-related industry advisories in 18 months
signals a systemic scope-qualification problem in the profession — a pattern that
Pre-Review's scope-language rules are designed to catch.

## 3. The $60,000 Question (page 4)

Since January 2026 the NZ Disputes Tribunal can consider claims up to $60,000 (doubled
from $30,000). Key features of the Tribunal that are unfavourable to engineers:

- Parties cannot be represented by a lawyer
- Decisions are based on "fairness", not contract terms
- The Tribunal can set aside a "full and final" settlement it considers unfair

The newsletter flagged the "blowback" risk explicitly: a $5,000 fee claim could trigger
a counterclaim for the full $60,000.

**Why it matters for Redline**: Raises the personal financial exposure for every report
quality failure — directly increases urgency for a documented Pre-Review audit trail.

## 4. Historical Case Study — Scope Qualification Failure (page 5)

Originally published in June 1979; reprinted in Issue 88.

**Facts**: An engineer was asked to report on a site with a cost-limited investigation.
Three hand-auger holes were placed; the report was favourable. When construction began,
extensive loose fill was found at locations away from the test holes. The owner sued for
negligence.

**Lesson (CEAS's words)**:

> "The Engineer should have fully qualified their report by pointing out the limits of
> the investigation and saying that the report's conclusion was based on this
> information only."

**Why it matters for Redline**: The exact failure mode Pre-Review's SCOPE-CLAUSE rules
are designed to catch — conclusions that outrun the evidence without qualification.
CEAS reprinting this in 2026 alongside the AI section suggests they see a structural
connection, even if they do not state it explicitly.
