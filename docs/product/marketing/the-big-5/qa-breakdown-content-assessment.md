## 1. Independently Groundable Insights

The following insights are grounded in Graeme's 25+ years of practitioner experience,
published industry literature, and discovery interviews. These are observable industry
realities.

### Insight 1: Senior reviewers spend disproportionate time on basic corrections

**Independent grounding**: Graeme can attest from direct experience. Any PD or Technical
Director at a mid-to-large consultancy will confirm this. It is a universal complaint
at industry conferences and CPD events. This is lived experience.

**Usable in content**: Yes, if sourced from Graeme's practitioner voice or discovery
interview quotes (with permission).

### Insight 2: Digital review checklists get low adoption

**Independent grounding**: The paper-to-digital transition is a shared experience across
every firm over 15 years old. Low adoption of digital forms is widely discussed in
construction technology literature. Graeme can speak to this from multiple firms.

**Usable in content**: Yes, as a general industry observation. Do not reference a
specific firm's adoption rate.

### Insight 3: Trust replaces process as QA breaks down

**Independent grounding**: "I know my team" as a substitute for formal review is a
recognised anti-pattern in quality management literature (not specific to civil
engineering). Graeme can attest to seeing this across multiple organisations.

**Usable in content**: Yes, as a general management / quality principle. Strong angle.

### Insight 4: Disciplines with standard templates produce more consistent reports

**Independent grounding**: Observable fact. Environmental and water engineering reports
are widely recognised as more templated than geotechnical. Graeme can confirm from
cross-discipline experience.

**Usable in content**: Yes. Strong angle for Skeleton Generator content.

### Insight 5: The "Swiss cheese" risk — the last reviewer might miss the error too

**Independent grounding**: James Reason's Swiss Cheese Model is published, peer-reviewed
safety science. Applying it to report review is a legitimate analytical frame.
Graeme can speak to the professional liability angle.

**Usable in content**: Yes. Strong angle for risk-focused and PI insurance content.

### Insight 6: The industry needs embedded QA, not more forms

**Independent grounding**: "Don't add a checklist, embed the check in the workflow" is a
well-established principle in quality engineering (poka-yoke, mistake-proofing). Graeme
can frame this as a practitioner observation. Published literature on construction
technology supports this.

**Usable in content**: Yes. This is the core Redline thesis.

## 6. Risks

### Risk 1: Making product claims we cannot yet deliver

The insights validate three Redline products (Skeleton Generator, Pre-Review, Audit
Trail). As of today, none of these are launched. Do not write content that implies they
exist and are available. Content should be problem-aware (the QA breakdown) not
solution-aware (Redline fixes it) until we have a shipping product.

**Rule**: Phase 1 content (pre-launch) focuses on the problem. Phase 2 content
(post-launch) connects the problem to Redline's solution.

### Risk 2: Shaming the industry

The tone must be empathetic, not accusatory. Our audience IS these engineers. If they
feel attacked, they will reject the message.

**Rule**: The editorial angle is "this is a shared industry challenge" not "look how bad
things are." Every headline must pass the test: would Anna share this on LinkedIn
without feeling embarrassed?
