## The Asset

The founder holds 15 years of Ground Engineering magazine (2014–2026), loaded entirely
into NotebookLM. This enables cross-archive querying across all issues simultaneously.

The asset gives Redline three things no other entrant in the NZ/AU geotech AI space
currently possesses:

1. **Cyclical pattern recognition** — problems that surface, disappear, and resurface
   across the industry cycle. The industry's "unsolved problems" reveal themselves
   through repetition.
2. **Industry memory** — comprehensive recall of the industry's own published conversation
   over 15 years. Senior engineers have deep project memory; they rarely have this breadth
   across the industry's published discourse.
3. **Unsolved-problem detection** — if the same category of problem recurs across 15 years,
   the industry has not found a systemic fix. Those are the highest-value targets for both
   content and product development.

## The Invisibility Protocol

The archive is never cited as a personal asset. This is binding on every external surface.

**Why:** In a market of approximately 2,000 practitioners who know each other, revealing
"I have 15 years of Ground Engineering" invites credential stress-testing ("Why? You're
not an engineer."), makes the source replicable by any competitor with a library
subscription, and reads as credential-seeking in a market where the founder's positioning
is explicitly that of a data scientist, not an engineer.

**Rules:**

1. **Never cite the magazine as a personal corpus.** Do not say "I read in Ground
   Engineering that..." or "according to a 2016 Ground Engineering article..." Repeated
   direct citation reveals systematic coverage. Any diligent person might have read a
   specific article; no one expects a non-engineer to hold 15 years of trade press.

2. **Do cite specific publicly available articles when it adds credibility.** This is a
   permitted framing in `positioning.md`. The distinction is citing one specific article
   (permitted) vs. revealing systematic multi-year coverage (not permitted).

3. **Frame as pattern observation.** "A pattern I keep seeing in how the industry
   discusses this problem..." not "Ground Engineering reported in 2016 that...".

4. **Let Graeme be the credibility source for technical claims.** The archive identifies
   topics; Graeme validates technical accuracy. The audience sees a technically reviewed
   post. They do not need to know how the topic was selected.

5. **Use the archive to ask better questions, not to provide answers.** Asking a senior
   engineer "what's your experience of how firms have handled [X] over the past decade?"
   generates more engagement than citing what the archive contains, and is consistent with
   the founder's intersection persona.

## Query Track A — Marketing Intelligence (Monthly)

**Owner:** Founder + John + media team.
**Cadence:** Monthly, triggered by each new Ground Engineering issue.
**Calendar entry:** See `docs/product/operations/cadences.md` → Monthly Editorial Session.

### Query sequence

1. **Resonance query:** "What topics in this issue have appeared in previous issues? How
   has the framing or proposed solution changed over time?"
   → Surfaces cyclical patterns → Big 5 Problems posts.

2. **Novelty query:** "What topics in this issue have NOT appeared in any previous issue?"
   → Surfaces genuine new developments → early-mover content opportunities.

3. **Commenting intelligence query:** For each active Dream 100 target's recent posts,
   query: "What has Ground Engineering published about [this topic] across the archive?"
   → Produces substantive commenting material for the 10:1 LCS rule.

4. **Big 5 filter:** Every topic maps to one of the five Big 5 categories. Topics that
   do not map are discarded. No exceptions.

### Session outputs

- 2–3 approved post angles per issue → queued in `editorial-calendar.md`.
- 1-paragraph product signal note → filed to Mark (Track B input).
- Updated Dream 100 commenting notes.

## NZ/AU Coverage Caveat

Ground Engineering is a UK-focused publication. The archive is reliable for:

- General geotechnical engineering quality problems (largely universal).
- Industry trend shifts and technology adoption patterns (largely universal).

The archive is **less reliable** for:

- NZ/AU-specific standards (NZS 3910, AS 4000, NZGS guidance).
- NZ/AU contract law and procurement practice.
- NZ/AU regulatory environment.

All Track B outputs require Graeme's NZ/AU applicability sign-off before entering any
product or strategy artifact.
