## Vision Statement

A neutral, infrastructure-grade quality layer for geotechnical engineering documents —
that catches what a senior engineer would catch, without replacing their judgement.

## What This Means

Redline is not a writing tool, not a chatbot, and not a regulator. It is the layer that
sits between the engineer and their report, applying firm-specific and jurisdictional
quality rules so that:

- A junior or intermediate engineer ships a draft that survives senior review with fewer rounds.
- A senior or principal engineer redirects review hours from line-checking to judgement.
- A firm's house rules become enforceable infrastructure, not tribal knowledge.

## Strategic Lexicon (Use Consistently)

- **Infrastructure, not SaaS** — Redline is plumbing for a regulated discipline. We are
  not a productivity hack. This framing protects pricing power and shapes buyer
  conversations away from per-seat tool-shopping.
- **Switzerland-neutral** — Redline does not take engineering positions, does not author
  opinions, does not arbitrate disputes between engineer and reviewer. It surfaces issues;
  the human resolves them. This is what makes the tool installable inside firms whose
  internal standards diverge.
- **Two modes, one product** — Pre-Review (junior-facing, helps them ship cleaner drafts)
  and Adversarial Scan (partner-facing, stress-tests final drafts against contractor or
  litigant attack patterns). Same engine, different posture.

## Five-Year Aspiration

Redline is the default quality layer running inside the document workflow of every
geotechnical consultancy in NZ and AU, with a defensible Standards Knowledge Store and
firm-specific House Rules registry that competitors cannot replicate without years of
domain investment.

In Geoffrey Moore's *Crossing the Chasm* terms, this is a beachhead-first aspiration:
NZ + AU geotechnical is the head-pin segment we intend to *dominate* (target: ≥ 50% of
new Pro-tier sales in the segment) before any bowling-pin expansion to adjacent
segments (structural, environmental, or other geographies). See `strategic-bets.md`
Bet 5.

## Disruption Frame (Christensen)

Redline is a **new-market disruption against nonconsumption**, not a low-end
disruption. Geotechnical consultancies do not currently buy software for the senior
review / quality-layer job — they consume senior engineer hours instead. The category
is nonconsumption; the wedge is making a new tool affordable and simple enough to
turn nonconsumers into consumers. Strategic implication: incumbents (Autodesk,
Bentley) are unlikely to attack early because the segment is invisible to their P&L.
