# Decision 004 — Mandatory Clause Boilerplate in Skeleton Generator

**Status**: Decided. **Date**: 2026-05-10. **Deciders**: Founder, Mark, Graeme.

---

## Context

The Skeleton Generator PRD (Feature A) specified that the generated skeleton includes "standard
clause references" and "placeholder text." The PRD did not name the mandatory clauses, did not
define the placeholder token format, and did not specify the source of the clause wording.

This left three open gaps at the engineering boundary:

1. Which clauses are mandatory for NZ/AU, which are conditional, and which are off by default?
2. What token syntax does the skeleton use for firm-specific placeholders?
3. Where does the clause wording come from — the Standards Knowledge Store, a curated list,
   or engineering judgment?

Graeme provided a domain-validated clause inventory and starting-point wording in a structured
analysis session (2026-05-10). This decision closes all three gaps.

## Decision

### 1. Mandatory Clause List (NZ/AU, Sprint 1)

| Clause ID | Name | Jurisdiction | Severity | When to include | Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| SCOPE-CLAUSE-01 | Inferred conditions caveat | NZ/AU | HIGH — mandatory | Always | NZ template [GRW] |
| SCOPE-CLAUSE-05 | Third-party reliance | NZ/AU | HIGH — mandatory | Always | NZ template [GRW] |
| SCOPE-CLAUSE-NEW | Temporal validity of recommendations | NZ/AU | MEDIUM | Always | NZ practice [RA] |
| SCOPE-CLAUSE-03 | Groundwater fluctuation | NZ/AU | MEDIUM — conditional | Only when a Groundwater section is present in the skeleton | NZ practice |
| SCOPE-CLAUSE-02 | Temporal boundaries | US | LOW — off by default NZ/AU | Inactive for NZ/AU | US ASCE practice |
| SCOPE-CLAUSE-04 | Changed conditions | US | LOW — off by default NZ/AU | Inactive for NZ/AU | US ASCE; replaced by "Further Work" section in NZ practice |
| SCOPE-CLAUSE-06 | Standard-of-care | US | LOW — off by default NZ/AU | Inactive for NZ/AU | US ASCE practice |

US clauses (02, 04, 06) are authored in the rule schema per Decision 003 but do not appear in the
NZ/AU skeleton. They become active when jurisdiction metadata is set to `US`.

### 2. Starting-Point Clause Wording

Graeme provided starting-point wording for the three mandatory NZ/AU clauses. This wording must
be validated by Graeme before shipping to production; it is not the final form.

**SCOPE-CLAUSE-01 — Inferred conditions caveat:**
> "Recommendations and opinions in this report are based on data from discrete investigation
> locations. The nature and continuity of subsoil away from these locations are inferred but it
> must be appreciated that actual conditions could vary from the assumed model."

**SCOPE-CLAUSE-05 — Third-party reliance:**
> "This report has been prepared for the exclusive use of [CLIENT_NAME]. No liability or
> responsibility whatsoever, in contract, tort or otherwise, is accepted to any third party who
> may use or rely on this report or any part of it without our prior written agreement."

**SCOPE-CLAUSE-NEW — Temporal validity of recommendations:**
> "If the project is modified in any significant way, or if the project is not initiated within
> eighteen months of the date of this report, [FIRM_NAME] should be given an opportunity to
> confirm that the recommendations are still valid."

### 3. Placeholder Token Format

Square bracket style, all-caps, underscore-separated words:

| Token | Maps to |
|---|---|
| `[CLIENT_NAME]` | Client name extracted from the Letter of Engagement |
| `[FIRM_NAME]` | Engineering firm name (from SSO profile or firm configuration) |
| `[PROJECT_NUMBER]` | Project number extracted from the Letter of Engagement |
| `[SITE_ADDRESS]` | Site address extracted from the Letter of Engagement |

Tokens are pre-populated by the LOE extraction where data is available. Where extraction fails,
the token renders verbatim in the DOCX — the engineer edits it in Word. This is consistent with
the existing LOE extraction behaviour (AC 8 in the skeleton PRD: "If LLM extraction cannot
populate a metadata field, the skeleton generates with that field blank").

### 4. Source of Clause Wording

Clause wording is maintained as a curated, versioned list inside the Standards Knowledge Store
(Feature N). It is not generated by the LLM at skeleton creation time. The LLM selects which
clauses apply (based on jurisdiction and skeleton structure); the wording is retrieved verbatim
from the curated list.

This ensures clause text is auditable, consistent across all skeletons, and not subject to LLM
paraphrase variation.

## Options Considered

| Option | Description | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| A. Defer clause list to Sprint 2 | Ship the skeleton without mandatory boilerplate; engineer adds clauses manually | Rejected — the inferred conditions caveat (SCOPE-CLAUSE-01) and third-party reliance (SCOPE-CLAUSE-05) are core risk-management content for every NZ/AU GBR/GIR. A skeleton without them ships a liability gap. |
| B. LLM-generated clause wording | Let the LLM generate clause text at skeleton creation time | Rejected — LLM paraphrase variation makes clause text non-auditable and non-comparable across documents. Curated list is required. |
| C. Placeholder tokens only, no clause text | Ship tokens without starting-point wording | Rejected — a blank placeholder is not a useful skeleton. The engineer needs a starting point to edit, not a prompt to author from scratch. |
| D. Graeme-validated curated list (adopted) | Curated, versioned clause wording; mandatory clauses auto-included; conditional clauses triggered by skeleton structure | **Adopted** |

## Rationale

- **Liability gap is immediate.** A GBR or GIR skeleton without SCOPE-CLAUSE-01 or
  SCOPE-CLAUSE-05 is structurally deficient for NZ/AU practice. Shipping without them would
  require Redline to disclaim its own output — undermining the product's core value.
- **Conditional inclusion is well-defined.** SCOPE-CLAUSE-03 (groundwater) is included
  whenever the Groundwater section is present. The skeleton already knows whether a
  Groundwater section is included; the trigger condition is unambiguous.
- **Token format consistency.** A single token syntax (`[ALL_CAPS]`) is parseable,
  visually distinct in Word, and unambiguous when LOE extraction pre-populates fields.
- **Curated source is the correct architecture.** Clause wording sourced from a curated list
  in the Standards Knowledge Store (Feature N) is consistent with the citation-only,
  internal-only architecture established in ADR-005. The LLM selects clauses; it does not
  author them.

## Consequences

- Feature A (Skeleton Generator) acceptance criteria are updated to include mandatory clause
  inclusion as a verifiable requirement.
- Feature N (Standards Knowledge Store) must include a curated clause wording table as part
  of its minimum viable subset.
- Graeme must validate final clause wording before Sprint 1 ships to production.
- The three US-practice clauses (02, 04, 06) are present in the clause schema with
  `enabled_by_default: false` for NZ/AU per Decision 003 — they do not appear in the skeleton.
