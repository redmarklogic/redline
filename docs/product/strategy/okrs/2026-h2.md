## Founder's Ask vs. Ron's Recommendation

**Founder asked for**: *5 paying customers in 3 months.*

**Ron's pushback**: That single number is a vanity threshold. With zero product
shipped on day 1, no churn definition, no unit-economic floor, and no leading
indicator, the metric protects nothing. The recommended structure below preserves
the ambition (5 paid conversions remains the lagging KR) but adds:

1. A leading indicator that fires in week 4, not week 12 (free-tier signups).
2. A definition of "paying" that prevents counting a $20-once charge as victory.
3. A retention qualifier that prevents declaring success on a customer who churns in week 13.
4. A qualified-conversation KR so a slow-starting funnel still produces evidence (LOIs).

**Decision required from founder**: accept this split, or override with the original
single-number target. If overridden, this file is updated to match and the leading
indicator is moved to a separate `leading-indicators.md`.

## Objective

Prove the wedge-to-paid funnel for Redline at small scale, in NZ and AU only, without
paid acquisition spend.

## Key Results

### KR1 — Free-tier signal (leading indicator, due Month 2)

By **2026-08-01** (60 days from launch), achieve **≥ 50 verified-email signups** on the free
Skeleton Generator, **AND** the weekly cohort signup-to-activation rate is ≥ 60%
(measured per weekly cohort, not as a cumulative average).

- *Definition of signup*: Google or Microsoft OAuth completes; work email captured;
  user generates ≥ 1 skeleton (not just signs up).
- *Why two numbers*: Per Eric Ries (*The Lean Startup*), a cumulative "50 signups"
  number alone is a vanity metric — any growing funnel will hit it eventually and
  reveal nothing about whether the product is changing user behaviour. Pairing the
  absolute count with a per-cohort activation rate satisfies the "3 As" of actionable
  metrics (Actionable, Accessible, Auditable) by exposing whether week-over-week
  product changes are improving the funnel.
- *Why this matters*: If KR1 fails, the wedge has no demand and no later KR can
  recover. Bet 1 trips its kill criterion.

### KR2 — Founder-led qualified conversations (Month 1–3)

By **2026-09-01** (90 days from launch), the founder has run **≥ 15 qualified discovery
conversations** with intermediate engineers or their Principals, each producing a
documented Switch timeline and Four-Forces map per `discovery-guide.md`.

- *Definition of qualified*: ≥ 30 minutes, work-email-verified, employed at an NZ or AU
  geotechnical consultancy, drafted at least one GBR/GIR in the last 12 months,
  AND the conversation produced a Switch timeline + Four-Forces map (raises
  signal-per-interview, per Christensen / Moesta).
- *Earlyvangelist tracker*: Of the 15, count how many meet Steve Blank's earlyvangelist
  criteria (knows they have the problem, actively looking, has built a workaround,
  has access to budget). If 0/15 are earlyvangelists, the funnel hypothesis is broken
  even if the count hits 15. Logged not as a KR threshold but as a diagnostic.
- *Velocity tension*: Steve Blank's Customer Development methodology assumes 10–15
  interviews *per week*, not per quarter. KR2's 1-per-week cadence is below Blank's
  velocity floor. We mitigate via interview depth (Switch + Four-Forces per call),
  but the tension is contested — see `decisions/parked-decisions.md` P-017.
- *Why this matters*: WTP interviews per Ramanujam framework — this is *parallel* to
  build, not sequential. Output feeds `pricing-methodology.md` validation.

### KR3 — Paid conversion (lagging, due Month 3)

By **2026-09-01** (90 days from launch), achieve **≥ 5 paying customers OR ≥ 2 firm-level LOIs
(combined value ≥ $10k ARR)**.

- *Definition of paying*: Active subscription, credit card on file, second invoice
  paid (not just trial-to-card conversion). Excludes founder's friends-and-family
  comps.
- *Definition of LOI*: Signed letter from a Principal or Partner naming Redline,
  identifying users, and stating intended usage. Not a verbal commitment.
- **Conversion offer is Pre-Review, not skeleton-only.** The founder's stated doubt
  (2026-04-18) — "I am not sure anyone will pay for a subscription that only creates
  skeleton documents" — is correct. The skeleton wedge generates demand and surfaces
  intent; the *paid* offer is the Pre-Review trial demonstrated on the customer's own
  in-flight document during the 30-minute discovery call. Counting a hypothetical
  skeleton-only subscription against KR3 is forbidden — it would falsify the wrong
  bet.
- *Why this matters*: Founder's original ask, with anti-vanity guardrails.

### KR4 — Retention qualifier (lagging, due Month 4)

By **2026-10-01** (120 days from launch), of the paying customers acquired in KR3, **≥ 60% are
still active in their second billing cycle** with first-cycle usage > 0.

- *Why this matters*: Prevents declaring victory on a 5-customer cohort that churns
  100% in month 2.

### KR5 — Wedge unit economics (operational ceiling)

Maintain **fully-loaded wedge cost ≤ $1.00 per quota-exhausted free user**.

- *Definition*: All inference, hosting, and email-verification costs per user who
  exhausts their 3–5 doc quota. Excludes engineering time.
- *Why this matters*: Bet 1's "beats Google Ads" claim is unfalsifiable without a
  cost ceiling. If KR5 trips, the bet is dead even if signups are strong.

### KR6 — Cohort instrumentation in place (operational, due Month 1)

By **2026-07-01** (30 days from launch), every signup is observable via a privacy-respecting
product-analytics surface, with weekly cohort dashboards covering signup → first
upload → generation start → generation complete → download → quota event → return
visit. Each user record carries (a) the SSO-captured work email and (b) a
founder-maintained LinkedIn URL field, populated by manual cross-reference, that
lets the founder distinguish junior from senior signups before outbound.

- *Definition of done*: KR1 signup count is reproducible from the analytics surface
  (no manual spreadsheet reconciliation), and the founder can answer "of the last 20
  signups, how many are senior engineers at NZ/AU consultancies" in under 5 minutes.
- *Why this matters*: KR1, KR2, and KR3 all depend on cohort-level visibility. Without
  this in week 4, every later KR becomes a gut call. Specification of events,
  user-identification approach, privacy posture, and tooling is in
  `instrumentation.md`.
