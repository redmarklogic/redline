# Jobs To Be Done — Redline

**Status**: Draft v1. **Owner**: Ron, with handoff to Mark for problem-framer expansion.

## Core Job (Day-1 ICP — Intermediate Engineer)

> When I am drafting a Geotechnical Baseline Report or Geotechnical Interpretive Report
> against a client scope and a thicket of jurisdictional standards, I want a quality layer
> that flags what a senior reviewer would mark up — so I can ship a draft that survives
> review in fewer rounds and rebuild less of my evening.

**Job executor**: Intermediate civil/geotechnical engineer (3–7 years post-graduation),
working inside a NZ or AU consultancy on residential or light-commercial GBR/GIR work.

**Trigger event**: Draft is due, senior reviewer is bottlenecked, engineer has run out of
self-check options and is about to submit something they know is incomplete.

**Current alternatives** (and their pain):
- Submit and absorb the markup pain — costs evenings, junior morale, partner time.
- Self-review against the firm's checklist — checklists are stale, partial, and tribal.
- Ask a senior peer — peer is also bottlenecked.
- Use ChatGPT — has no jurisdictional grounding, no firm context, no audit trail; partner won't allow it.

**Success looks like**: Senior reviewer's first markup pass returns ≤ N comments instead
of 3N comments. Engineer learns the firm's standards by induction over time.

## Phase-2 Job (Principal / Partner)

> When I am responsible for a final-draft GBR going to a client or tender, I want an
> adversarial scan that simulates how a contractor's lawyer or a future claimant would
> attack the baseline language — so I can close the language gaps before they become
> a Differing Site Conditions claim.

**Job executor**: Principal engineer or technical Partner with sign-off authority and
litigation exposure. **Triggered by**: high-value contract, prior claim history, or a
known-hostile counterparty. **Currently solved by**: senior peer review and external legal
review — both expensive and slow. **Phase-2 because**: requires Adversarial Scan engine
maturity (see `parked-decisions.md`).

## How We Will Validate These Jobs

The JTBD statements above are hypotheses, not findings. They are validated through the
15 KR2 discovery conversations using the Switch interview structure (Christensen /
Moesta) and a Four-Forces map (Push, Pull, Anxiety, Habit). The structured interview
guide is `discovery-guide.md`. A KR2 conversation that does not produce a Switch
timeline and a Four-Forces map for the customer is rapport-building, not discovery.

## Jobs Redline Will Not Solve (See `non-goals.md`)

- Authoring engineering opinions or recommendations.
- Replacing the senior reviewer's judgement.
- Acting as an arbiter between engineer and reviewer.
- Compliance attestation or sign-off on behalf of a Chartered Professional Engineer.
