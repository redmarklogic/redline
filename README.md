# Redline

*by [Redmark Logic](https://redmarklogic.com)*

Redline is the neutral quality layer for geotechnical engineering documents — purpose-built for NZ and AU consultancies. It handles the routine compliance checks so senior reviewers can focus on the engineering judgment that matters. Firms' house rules and jurisdictional standards become enforceable infrastructure, not tribal knowledge.

## What Redline does

**Skeleton Generator** — Engineers upload a Letter of Engagement or scope document. Redline generates a structured report skeleton with the correct section hierarchy, report type (GBR, GIR), and NZ/AU jurisdictional alignment. Engineers start writing with the right structure; missing sections get caught before the draft begins.

**Pre-Review** — Before a draft reaches the senior reviewer, engineers run Pre-Review. Redline checks the document against the Standards Knowledge Store (NZS, AS, NZGS guidance), the firm's House Rules, and a rule engine covering taboo language, undefined acronyms, ambiguity flags, unit inconsistencies, citation correctness, and structural completeness. Flags are returned as inline comments — source-linked, scoped, and auditable. The reviewer receives a cleaner draft; the intermediate receives structured feedback before a human reads the file.

**Adversarial Scan** — For final drafts on contentious projects, partners and principals run Adversarial Scan. The same engine is reoriented to stress-test the document against the patterns a contractor's claim engineer would apply — Differing Site Conditions language, baseline ambiguity, missing caveats. Gaps are closed before the document leaves the firm.

**Audit Trail** — Every Redline session generates a signed, timestamped, exportable professional practice record — scoped to what was checked and explicitly bounded to what was not. The record survives legal discovery. It states on its face what was checked within scope and that engineering judgment remains the responsibility of the certifying engineer. This is the QA system answer when a PI insurer asks.

**House Rules** — Firms configure their own quality conventions — report templates, mandatory clause language, forbidden phrasings, client-specific requirements — inside Redline. House Rules are enforced at the point of writing, not documented somewhere nobody reads.

**Standards Knowledge Store** — The defensible asset underneath the product. Redline's rule engine is grounded in NZS, AS, NZGS guidance, and firm-specific House Rules — not a general-purpose language model's sense of geotechnical convention. When a standard updates, the rules update.

## Who it's for

NZ and AU geotechnical consultancies with 5–50 staff — enough report volume to feel the senior-review bottleneck, not enough budget for a dedicated QA manager.

**The engineer using it:** Intermediate, 3–7 years post-graduation, working toward CPEng. Drafts GBRs and GIRs independently. Wants fewer review comments and first-round approval without waiting for the reviewer's calendar.

**The firm buying it:** Technical Director or co-owner, CPEng. Manages senior engineers whose hours are consumed by copy-paste corrections and standards lookups instead of engineering judgment. Wants recovered senior capacity and a QA system they can describe to their PI insurer.

## What Redline will never do

- Author engineering opinions or draft interpretive sections
- Certify that a report meets any standard — the certifying engineer remains accountable
- Check calculations or perform design verification
- Cover structural, environmental, or civil drainage reports
- Train on customer documents — documents are read in-session, full stop

## Standards coverage

NZS 3910, AS 4000, AS/NZS 4122, NZGS guidance. NZ and AU only.
